Why Convert JPG to PNG?

PNG format is lossless, preserving every detail of your original image without any quality degradation. JPG compression removes information permanently, but PNG stores all original data. For archiving important images or creating graphics for professional use, PNG conversion is ideal. PNG also supports transparency (alpha channels), allowing you to create images with transparent backgrounds. Designers prefer PNG format for logos, icons, and graphics where precision is critical.

PNG is the superior choice for web graphics with text. Text remains crisp and sharp in PNG format, while JPG may show compression artifacts around letters. For images with sharp color boundaries and geometric shapes, PNG maintains perfect fidelity. The trade-off is file size—PNG files are typically larger than JPG versions. Use PNG for professional graphics, use JPG for photographs and images where compression is acceptable.
